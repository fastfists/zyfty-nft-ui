FREQUENTLY
ASKED
QUESTIONS

Q What happens if I buy an NFT but then decide I don’t want to own an NFT lease but would rather just own the title?
A An NFT owner can elect to redeem their NFT at any point with Zyfty. This will cause the NFT to be destroyed. See white paper and for details.
Q Can I look at the NFT home before I enter into the purchase process (before I bid)?
A Most likely, yes. However, much like the traditional sales process, the extent to which viewings will be offered is up to the seller of the NFT.
Q Is there an escrow process upon an offer being accepted to purchase an NFT?
A Yes, there is an escrow process that is very similar to the current marketplace. Upon an offer, or bid for the home, a small amount of currency will be held in escrow as a “soft” deposit. Upon acceptance of an offer by the seller, an additional amount will be added to the “soft” deposit along with a “hard” deposit later in the escrow period. Like all real estate transactions, the length of the escrow period as well as “soft” and “hard” deposit amounts vary based on the NFT seller’s purchasing criteria.
Q How do I receive information regarding the home?
A In addition to the information noted in the NFT home listing on the Zyfty Marketplace, potential NFT buyers may purchase a “manifest” which includes all documents necessary to perform due diligence on the property represented by the NFT.
Q How do I pay my taxes and any fees associated with my home?
A The NFT includes mechanisms to pay property taxes, HOA and other fees.
Q Can I make improvements and upgrades to my home?
A An NFT owner can make improvements to the home, just like traditional home ownership.
